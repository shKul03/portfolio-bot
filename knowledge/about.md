# About Minnie — Personal Style & Working Approach

Some people are naturally drawn toward the arts. Others gravitate toward science and engineering. I've spent most of my life refusing to choose between the two.

I've always been a high achiever academically. Throughout school and college, I consistently ranked among the top students, graduating from ICSE with 98% in my tenth-grade examinations and maintaining strong academic performance throughout my education. But what motivated me was never competition alone. What fascinated me was understanding. I loved discovering how things worked, breaking problems apart, finding patterns, and constructing solutions from first principles.

That curiosity eventually led me to Computer Science, a field that felt like a perfect home for the way my mind works. Programming was never just about writing code. It was about building systems, solving puzzles, and turning ideas into something tangible. Whether I'm designing a distributed system, experimenting with artificial intelligence, architecting a new product, or debugging an issue that refuses to reveal itself, I enjoy the process of investigation as much as the outcome.

I've always approached technology with a sense of play.

Artificial intelligence, in particular, captivates me because it feels like the world's largest puzzle. Every model, framework, and breakthrough opens the door to new possibilities. I enjoy exploring AI not only as a developer, but as a tinkerer. I love experimenting with new tools, testing ideas, building side projects, and understanding what happens beneath the surface. My relationship with technology is driven less by trends and more by genuine curiosity. I don't enjoy simply using things—I want to understand them.

That same curiosity extends far beyond engineering.

I've been an avid reader for as long as I can remember. Science fiction has always held a special place in my heart because it asks questions about humanity, technology, and the future. Young adult fiction appeals to the part of me that still believes growth, discovery, and adventure are worth chasing. Books have always been more than entertainment; they've been windows into new worlds and new ways of thinking.

Stories, in all forms, matter deeply to me.

I'm a passionate film and television enthusiast who appreciates thoughtful storytelling, memorable direction, and narratives that stay with you long after they've ended. I enjoy mysteries that reward careful observation, thrillers that keep me guessing, psychological horror that explores the darker corners of the human mind, and slice-of-life stories that quietly reveal profound truths about everyday life.

Some of my favorite works reflect that range. I love the ambitious ideas and scale of *Interstellar* and *Tenet*. I admire the intelligence and character work of *Sherlock*. I appreciate emotionally rich stories such as *When Life Gives You Tangerines* and *The Good Bad Mother*. Whether a story is exploring the end of the world, solving a mystery, or simply portraying ordinary people living ordinary lives, I'm drawn to narratives that feel meaningful.

Creativity has always been just as important to me as logic.

As a child, I spent countless hours sketching and painting, and art remains one of the ways I process ideas and express myself. I was also the lead singer in my school's choir, an experience that deepened my appreciation for music and performance. Creativity has never felt separate from engineering. To me, both disciplines involve the same fundamental act: creating something that didn't exist before.

Music, too, is woven into the fabric of my life. It accompanies almost everything I do—working, reading, traveling, thinking, and creating. My taste spans languages, genres, and moods because what attracts me isn't a category but a feeling. Music is one of the constants that grounds me no matter what phase of life I'm in.

Beyond academics, technology, and creativity, there is one characteristic that defines me more than anything else: I love exploring.

New ideas excite me. New technologies excite me. New places, new challenges, new experiences, and new adventures excite me. I rarely feel content staying within the boundaries of what I already know. Growth, for me, comes from stepping into unfamiliar territory and figuring things out along the way.

This mindset has shaped both my personal and professional life. It is why I pursue ambitious projects. It is why I enjoy learning new technologies even when they're outside my comfort zone. It is why I find myself diving into topics that have nothing to do with my formal education simply because they spark my interest.

At heart, I am an engineer, a creator, and a lifelong learner.

I love solving problems, but I also love telling stories. I enjoy analyzing systems, but I also appreciate art. I am equally fascinated by algorithms and human nature, by machine learning models and meaningful narratives, by scientific possibility and creative expression.

The common thread running through all of it is curiosity.

I approach the world the way a child approaches a puzzle—with excitement, persistence, imagination, and the belief that there is always something new waiting to be discovered.
